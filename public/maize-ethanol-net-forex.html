Maize ethanol: the net forex the crude-only story leaves out

The government counts the crude that maize ethanol displaces. It does not net off the maize India now imports to feed the same distilleries. This is the whole ledger: forex saved on crude, minus forex spent on feedstock. Push the import share and watch the saving disappear.

Why this is the honest ledger. Rice ethanol used FCI public stock, which India cannot export under the WTO peace clause, so its export counterfactual was a phantom. Maize is different. It is open-market, freely traded, and ethanol demand already turned India from Asia's top maize exporter into a net importer in 2024. So the feedstock cost here is real forex leaving the country, not a shadow price. Where crude sits low and the import share sits high, the programme's headline crude saving can go fully negative on the maize channel. Maize to ethanol about 7 to 13 MT in 2024-25, rising toward 27 MT by 2030 (NITI Aayog). Yield about 380 L/tonne. India imported 0.9 MT maize in 2024, exports fell to 0.5 MT from 1.9 MT. Import parity roughly $230 to $280/MT CIF, Ukraine and US corn cheapest, US-India deal adds duty-free US corn. Brent about $78/bbl, 8 July 2026. Crude displacement default 290 L/bbl is the energy-equivalent floor. Import share is a marginal assumption, not a measured split.
