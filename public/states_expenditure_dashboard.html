Revenue expenditure denominator is total revenue expenditure (Appendix-2). For any sector's Capital exp. view, "% of budget" is the item's share of total capital outlay (Appendix-4, pure asset-creating capex), not total capital disbursements, since disbursements also include gross discharge of internal debt, i.e. short-term borrowing rollovers that are not real spending and would otherwise swamp the ratio. Transport capital figures sum "Transport" and "Communications" sub-heads to match the combined revenue-side "Transport and Communications" head. Capex is total capital outlay itself; its "% of budget" is capex's share of total capital account disbursements, i.e. what fraction of the gross capital account is genuine asset creation versus debt rollover. Total govt. spending (capital view) is total capital disbursements including that debt rollover, so its "% of budget" is trivially 100% and is shown for GSDP comparison only. Own tax revenue is the state's own tax collection (Appendix-1), excluding its share of central taxes and grants; its "% of budget" denominator is total revenue receipts. Interest payments and pensions are Non-Developmental Expenditure (General Services) and have no capital-account equivalent, so only the revenue view is available for them. GSDP is current-price GSDP from MoSPI Table 21, available from 2011-12; earlier years of expenditure data exist in the source but are not shown here since they'd have no GSDP denominator. Gujarat GSDP is unavailable for 2023-24 in the source and is omitted rather than estimated.
