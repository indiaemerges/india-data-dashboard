Maize: export the grain or save the crude

The real opportunity cost of maize ethanol is not the trickle India imports. It is the forex the same maize would earn if it were exported instead. Unlike FCI rice, maize leaves the country freely, so this is a live choice. Both paths are priced at today's market.

Why export is the right lens. Maize is open-market and freely exported, so every tonne sent to a distillery is a tonne not shipped abroad. India exported about 1 MT of maize in the first five months of MY2025-26, roughly three times the year-earlier pace, on a record 55 MT crop, and USDA cut projected imports to just 50,000 tonnes. So the export alternative is real, not a phantom like FCI rice under the WTO peace clause. Caveat: India cannot dump the full ethanol volume abroad at $230 without softening the price, since its non-GMO maize mainly serves nearby buyers, so read the export bar as marginal opportunity value, not a guaranteed sum at scale.
